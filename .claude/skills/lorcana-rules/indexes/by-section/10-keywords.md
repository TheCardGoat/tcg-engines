# Section 10: Keywords

**Rule References:** 10.1 - 10.13
**Full Document:** `references/disney-lorcana-comprehensive-rules/Disney_Lorcana_Comprehensive_Rules_082225_EN_251019_220309.md` (Section 10)

## Overview
This section provides the technical definitions for all keyword abilities in Disney Lorcana. Keywords are shorthand for larger abilities.

---

## 10.1. General
**Summary:** How keywords work and general keyword rules
**Key terms:** keywords, stacking, technical definitions, reminder text
**Rule refs:** 10.1.1-10.1.x

**Rules:**
- Keywords are shorthand for larger abilities
- Reminder text (in italics) explains keywords but isn't rules text
- Reminder text may vary without changing the keyword meaning
- Some keywords stack (multiple instances add together)
- Each keyword has a technical definition in this section

---

## Keyword List

### 10.2. Bodyguard
**Summary:** Opponents must challenge this character first
**Key terms:** bodyguard, must challenge, protection
**Rule ref:** 10.2

**Effect:**
- Opponents can't challenge your other characters while you have a character with Bodyguard ready
- They must challenge your Bodyguard character (if they want to challenge)
- Only works while the Bodyguard character is ready

**Reminder Text:** "While you have a character with Bodyguard, opponents can't challenge your other characters."

**Interaction with Evasive:** Evasive characters can ignore Bodyguard and challenge other characters

---

### 10.3. Challenger
**Summary:** Deal more damage while challenging
**Key terms:** challenger, increased damage, while challenging, stacking
**Rule ref:** 10.3

**Effect:**
- This character gets +X Strength while challenging
- Increases damage dealt during the challenge damage step
- Only applies when this character is the one challenging (not when being challenged)

**Stacking (10.3.x):** Multiple instances of Challenger add together
- Example: Challenger +2 and Challenger +1 = Challenger +3 total

**Reminder Text:** "This character gets +X Strength while challenging."

---

### 10.4. Evasive
**Summary:** Can challenge ready characters and ignores Bodyguard
**Key terms:** evasive, challenge ready, ignore bodyguard
**Rule ref:** 10.4

**Effect:**
- This character can challenge ready characters (normally can only challenge exerted)
- This character can challenge any character, ignoring Bodyguard

**Reminder Text:** "This character can challenge ready characters and ignores Bodyguard."

**Strategic Note:** Very powerful for aggressive play, bypasses normal challenge restrictions

---

### 10.5. Reckless
**Summary:** This character can't quest
**Key terms:** reckless, can't quest, challenge only
**Rule ref:** 10.5

**Effect:**
- This character cannot quest
- Can still challenge, use abilities, etc.
- Usually found on aggressive characters with high strength

**Reminder Text:** "This character can't quest."

---

### 10.6. Resist
**Summary:** Take less damage from all sources
**Key terms:** resist, damage reduction, stacking
**Rule ref:** 10.6

**Effect:**
- This character takes X less damage from all sources
- Applies to damage dealt (not damage put)
- Applies to challenge damage and ability damage

**Stacking (10.6.x):** Multiple instances add together
- Example: Resist +1 and Resist +2 = Resist +3 total

**O Damage (10.6.x):** If damage would be reduced below 0, the character takes 0 damage

**Reminder Text:** "This character takes X less damage."

**Important:** Only affects "dealt" damage, not "put damage counters"

---

### 10.7. Rush
**Summary:** Can quest/challenge/use abilities the turn played
**Key terms:** rush, no dry requirement, immediate use
**Rule ref:** 10.7

**Effect:**
- This character doesn't need to be dry (can act the turn it's played)
- Can quest, challenge, and use exert abilities immediately
- Bypasses the normal "summoning sickness" rule

**Reminder Text:** "This character can quest, challenge, or use abilities the turn they're played."

---

### 10.8. Shift
**Summary:** Play this character on top of another for an alternate cost
**Key terms:** shift, alternate cost, stack, top card, under
**Rule ref:** 10.8

**Effect:**
- Alternative way to play this character
- Choose a character in play with matching name and cost ≤ Shift cost
- Pay the Shift cost instead of ink cost
- Place this character on top of the chosen character (creating a stack)
- The new character is ready (even if the one underneath was exerted)
- The new character keeps any damage from the character underneath

**Rules from 10.8:**
- **Alternate Cost** - Shift is an alternate way to play the card
- **State** - Shifted character is ready regardless of the underneath character's state
- **Damaged** - Damage carries over from underneath character
- **Leaving Play** - If shifted character leaves, all cards in stack leave together
- **Variants** - Different art/versions of same name work for Shift

**Reminder Text:** "You may play this character on top of a character named [X] for [cost] ink."

**Example:** "Shift 4" means you can play this for 4 ink if you put it on top of a character with a matching name

---

### 10.9. Singer
**Summary:** This character can sing songs for you
**Key terms:** singer, songs, exert to sing, alternate cost
**Rule ref:** 10.9

**Effect:**
- You may exert this character to sing a song
- The song must have ink cost ≤ this character's cost
- Singing is an alternate cost (don't pay ink)
- Character must be dry to sing (like other exert abilities)

**Reminder Text:** "This character counts as cost [X] to sing songs."

**How to Sing:**
1. Announce you're playing the song
2. Exert a Singer character with cost ≥ song's cost
3. Song effect resolves
4. Song goes to discard

---

### 10.10. Sing Together
**Summary:** Multiple characters can sing a song together
**Key terms:** sing together, multiple singers, alternate cost, triggered abilities
**Rule ref:** 10.10

**Effect:**
- You may exert two or more characters to sing a song together
- Combined cost of exerted characters must be ≥ song's cost
- Characters must be dry
- Alternate cost for playing the song

**Alternate Cost (10.10.x):** Singing together is an alternate way to pay for songs

**Resolving Triggered Abilities (10.10.x):** If characters have "when exerted" abilities, those trigger and resolve after the song

**Reminder Text:** "This character counts as cost [X] when singing songs with other characters."

---

### 10.11. Support
**Summary:** Give another character +1 Strength this turn
**Key terms:** support, temporary boost, this turn, choose character
**Rule ref:** 10.11

**Effect:**
- When you play this character, choose one of your other characters
- That character gets +1 Strength for the rest of this turn
- Effect ends at end of turn

**Reminder Text:** "When you play this character, you may choose one of your other characters. That character gets +1 Strength this turn."

**Note:** Temporary effect, doesn't persist

---

### 10.12. Vanish
**Summary:** Banish this card when it leaves play
**Key terms:** vanish, banish instead, different zone
**Rule ref:** 10.12

**Effect:**
- If this card would go to any zone other than play, banish it instead
- Means the card goes to discard instead of hand, deck, etc.
- Prevents bouncing to hand or putting on top of deck

**Reminder Text:** "If this card would go to any zone other than play, banish it instead."

---

### 10.13. Ward
**Summary:** Can't be chosen by opponent's effects
**Key terms:** ward, can't be chosen, protection, opponent effects
**Rule ref:** 10.13

**Effect:**
- Opponents can't choose this card with their abilities or effects
- Can still be affected by "all characters" or "each character" effects
- Can still be challenged
- Only protects from targeted effects

**Reminder Text:** "Opponents can't choose this card with abilities."

**What Ward Prevents:**
- "Choose a character and banish it"
- "Choose a character. It gets -2 Strength"
- Any effect that requires opponent to "choose" this card

**What Ward Doesn't Prevent:**
- "Banish all characters"
- "Each character gets -1 Strength"
- Challenges (can still be challenged normally)
- Your own effects

---

## Keyword Stacking Summary

**Keywords That Stack:**
- **Challenger** - Multiple instances add together (Challenger +2 and +1 = +3)
- **Resist** - Multiple instances add together (Resist +1 and +2 = +3)

**Keywords That Don't Stack:**
- Most other keywords don't stack - having multiple instances doesn't do anything additional
- Examples: Bodyguard, Evasive, Reckless, Rush, Singer, Ward, Vanish

---

## Common Keyword Interactions

### Evasive vs Bodyguard
- Evasive ignores Bodyguard
- Evasive character can challenge any character, even if opponent has ready Bodyguard

### Resist vs Challenger
- Challenger increases damage dealt
- Resist reduces damage taken
- Calculate net damage: (Base Strength + Challenger) - Resist

### Rush vs Dry Requirement
- Rush bypasses the dry requirement
- Character with Rush can quest/challenge/use ⬇ abilities immediately

### Shift and Damage
- Damage carries over when shifting
- If you shift onto a character with 2 damage, the new character has 2 damage

### Ward vs Targeted Effects
- Ward only prevents "choose" effects
- "All characters" or "each character" effects still work
- Challenges still work (challenging isn't "choosing")

## Common Questions This Section Answers

**Q: Can I challenge a ready character?**
A: Only if your character has Evasive (10.4)

**Q: Does Bodyguard work when exerted?**
A: No, only while ready (10.2)

**Q: Can Evasive characters ignore Bodyguard?**
A: Yes (10.4)

**Q: Does damage from Shift carry over?**
A: Yes (10.8)

**Q: Can a character with Reckless quest?**
A: No (10.5)

**Q: Does Resist work against "put damage counters"?**
A: No, only against dealt damage (10.6)

**Q: Can I use a character with Rush immediately?**
A: Yes, the turn it's played (10.7)

**Q: Does Ward prevent challenges?**
A: No, only targeted "choose" effects (10.13)

**Q: How does Sing Together work?**
A: Exert multiple characters, their costs add together (10.10)
