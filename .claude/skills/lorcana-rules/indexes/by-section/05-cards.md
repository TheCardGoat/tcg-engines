## Overview
This section covers the various conditions and states that cards can be in during gameplay.

---

## 5.1. Conditions
**Summary:** The different states cards can have while in play
**Key terms:** ready, exerted, damaged, undamaged, under, on top, stack
**Rule refs:** 5.1.1-5.1.7

---

### Ready (5.1.1)
**Summary:** The default state for cards - able to be used
**Key terms:** ready, enter play, exert symbol

**Details:**
- Cards enter play ready (vertical orientation)
- Can't use abilities with exert symbol (⬇) as part of the cost if the card is not ready
- Ready characters can quest, challenge, or use exert abilities

---

### Exerted (5.1.2)
**Summary:** Cards that have been turned sideways after use
**Key terms:** exerted, turned sideways, exert, abilities without exert cost

**Details:**
- When a card is exerted, it's turned sideways (horizontal orientation)
- Can still use abilities that don't require ⬇ as part of the cost
- Can't quest, challenge, or use ⬇ abilities while exerted
- Readies during the Ready step of your Beginning Phase

**How cards become exerted:**
- Questing with a character
- Challenging with a character
- Using an ability with ⬇ in the cost
- Paying ink cost (exerting ink cards)
- Being challenged (target must be exerted)

---

### Damaged (5.1.3)
**Summary:** Cards that have at least one damage counter on them
**Key terms:** damaged, damage counter, at least 1 damage

**Details:**
- A card with 1 or more damage is considered damaged
- Damage persists - it doesn't go away at end of turn
- Some effects reference whether a card is damaged
- See Section 9 for detailed damage rules

---

### Undamaged (5.1.4)
**Summary:** Cards that have no damage counters
**Key terms:** undamaged, no damage, 0 damage

**Details:**
- A card with 0 damage is considered undamaged
- This is the default state for cards entering play
- Some effects specifically check if a card is undamaged

---

### Under (5.1.5)
**Summary:** Cards that have one or more cards stacked on top of them
**Key terms:** under, stack, top card, can't choose

**Details:**
- A card with one or more cards on top of it is "under" the top card
- Can't choose or interact with cards that are under the top card
- Most commonly occurs with Shift ability
- Cards under are still in play but can't be targeted

---

### On Top (5.1.6)
**Summary:** Cards that are placed on top of other cards in play
**Key terms:** on top, stack, top card, doesn't gain text

**Details:**
- A card on top of one or more cards is "on top" of all cards under it
- The card on top doesn't gain the text of cards under it
- The card on top is called the "top card" in a stack
- Only the top card can be chosen/targeted

---

### In a Stack (5.1.7)
**Summary:** When multiple cards are layered on top of each other in play
**Key terms:** stack, top card, under, leave play together

**Details:**
- When a card is placed on top of one or more cards, they form a stack
- Cards are only "in a stack" while in play
- If the top card leaves play, ALL cards in the stack move to the same zone
- After leaving play, cards are no longer in a stack

**Most common use:** Shift ability - shifting character goes on top of another character

---

## Visual Representation

**Ready:** Card is vertical (upright)
**Exerted:** Card is horizontal (turned sideways)
**Damaged:** Has damage counters placed on it
**Stack:** Cards layered on top of each other, only top card is visible/targetable

## Common Questions This Section Answers

**Q: Can I use an exerted character's abilities?**
A: Yes, as long as the ability doesn't require ⬇ as part of the cost (5.1.2)

**Q: Do I ready my cards at the start of my turn?**
A: Yes, in the Ready step of your Beginning Phase (see Section 4.2.1)

**Q: Does damage go away at the end of turn?**
A: No, damage is persistent and stays on the card (5.1.3)

**Q: If I Shift onto a character, can I use the abilities of the character underneath?**
A: No, the top card doesn't gain the text of cards under it (5.1.6)

**Q: Can I target a character that's under another character?**
A: No, you can't choose cards that are under the top card in a stack (5.1.5)

**Q: If a shifted character is banished, what happens to the cards underneath?**
A: All cards in the stack move to the same zone (discard) together (5.1.7)
