# Section 6: Card Types

**Rule References:** 6.1 - 6.5
**Full Document:** `references/disney-lorcana-comprehensive-rules/Disney_Lorcana_Comprehensive_Rules_082225_EN_251019_220309.md` (lines 434-540)

## Overview
This section covers the four types of cards in Disney Lorcana: Characters, Actions, Items, and Locations, along with their specific rules and card anatomy.

---

## 6.1. Characters
**Summary:** Cards that can be in play, quest, and challenge
**Key terms:** characters, quest, challenge, strength, willpower, lore value, dry, classifications
**Rule refs:** 6.1.1-6.1.4

### What Makes a Character:
A card is a character if it has:
1. Both Strength (⚔) and Willpower (⬨) values (6.1.2.1)
2. At least one classification from the character list (6.1.2.2)

**Character classifications include:** Alien, Ally, Broom, Captain, Deity, Detective, Dragon, Dreamborn, Entangled, Fairy, Floodborn, Hero, Hyena, Illusion, Inventor, King, Knight, Madrigal, Mentor, Musketeer, Pirate, Prince, Princess, Puppy, Queen, Racer, Robot, Seven Dwarfs, Sorcerer, Storyborn, Tigger, Titan, Villain

### Key Rules:
- Only characters can quest or challenge (6.1.3)
- Characters must be "dry" (in play since beginning of Set step) to quest, challenge, or use ⬇ abilities (6.1.4)
- In play = "character", in other zones = "character card" (6.1.1)

## 6.2. Parts of a Card
**Summary:** Anatomy of a card and what each part means
**Key terms:** name, version, full name, ink type, cost, inkwell symbol, strength, willpower, lore value, classifications, abilities
**Rule refs:** 6.2.1-6.2.14

### Card Components:

#### Name (6.2.4)
**Summary:** The main name of the card (larger print)
**Details:**
- Effects that look for a "name" only look at this line, ignore version
- Example: "Peter Pan" matches both "Peter Pan - Fearless Fighter" and "Peter Pan - Never Land Hero"

**Characters with Two Names (6.2.4.1-6.2.4.2):**
- Some characters have an ampersand (&) between two names
- Example: "Flotsam & Jetsam" is both "Flotsam" and "Jetsam"
- Effects looking for either name will find this character
- Still counts as a single character

#### Version (6.2.5)
**Summary:** Differentiates cards with the same name
**Details:**
- Example: "Ice Queen" or "Snow Queen" for Elsa cards
- Different versions are different cards

#### Full Name (6.2.4)
**Summary:** Name + Version together
**Details:**
- Example: "Elsa - Ice Queen" (full name)
- Used for the 4-copy deck limit (Rule 2.1.1.3)
- Different versions of same character count as different cards

#### Ink Type (6.2.3)
**Summary:** The card's color/ink type
**Details:**
- Identified by ink type symbol
- Colored bar behind card name shows the color
- Some cards have two ink types (dual-ink cards) with two symbols (6.2.3.1)
- Important for deck building (max 2 ink types per deck)

#### Card Cost (6.2.7)
**Summary:** Amount of ink needed to play the card
**Details:**
- Number in top left corner
- Paid by exerting ink cards from your inkwell

#### Inkwell Symbol (6.2.8)
**Summary:** Swirl icon (⬡) indicating card can go into inkwell
**Details:**
- Appears around the card's cost
- If present, card can be put into inkwell
- Cards in inkwell count as 1 ink each

#### Strength (⚔) (6.2.9)
**Summary:** How much damage this character deals in challenges
**Details:**
- Only appears on characters
- Used when challenging or being challenged
- If Strength is 0 or less, character deals no damage
- Can be modified by effects (e.g., Challenger, Support)

#### Willpower (⬨) (6.2.10)
**Summary:** How much damage this character can take before being banished
**Details:**
- Only appears on characters
- When damage ≥ willpower, character is banished (game state check)
- Damage persists across turns

#### Lore Value (◊) (6.2.11)
**Summary:** How much lore the player gains when character quests
**Details:**
- Only appears on characters and some locations
- Gained when character quests (Section 4.3.5)
- Locations with ◊ provide lore during Set step (Section 4.2.2)

#### Classifications (6.2.6)
**Summary:** Categories that identify card characteristics
**Details:**
- Examples: Hero, Villain, Princess, Pirate, etc.
- May be referenced by card effects
- Example: "This character gets +1 Strength for each Villain you have in play"

#### Abilities (6.2.12)
**Summary:** Special effects and powers the card has
**Details:**
- Appear in text box
- May have story names (used for reference only)
- Keywords are shorthand for abilities (Section 10)
- See Section 7 for detailed ability rules

#### Non-Gameplay Elements:
- **Art** (6.2.2) - Visual only, not used for gameplay
- **Flavor Text** (6.2.13) - Thematic text, not used for gameplay
- **Ancillary Information** (6.2.14) - Artist name, collector number, set number, language, legal text, rarity symbol

## 6.3. Actions
**Summary:** Cards that can be played but can't stay in play
**Key terms:** action, effect, songs, resolve immediately, discard
**Rule refs:** 6.3.1-6.3.4

### Key Rules:
- Actions have "Action" on classification line (6.3.1.1)
- Played from hand but not considered "in play" (6.3.1.2)
- Effects resolve immediately, then card goes to discard (Section 4.3.4.7)
- Action effects don't use the bag (6.3.1.2)
- Actions don't have Strength, Willpower, or Lore values

### Songs (6.3.3-6.3.4)
**Summary:** Special type of action that can be "sung"
**Details:**
- Songs have "Action - Song" on classification line (6.3.3)
- Can be played normally by paying ink cost
- Can also be sung by exerting a character with cost ≥ song's cost (6.3.4)
- Singing is an alternate cost, not a triggered ability
- See Section 10.9 (Singer) for details

---

## 6.4. Items
**Summary:** Cards that stay in play and provide ongoing benefits or activated abilities
**Key terms:** item, activated abilities, enter play ready
**Rule refs:** 6.4.1-6.4.3

### Key Rules:
- Items have "Item" on classification line (6.4.1)
- Can have activated abilities (6.4.2)
- Can use activated abilities the same turn they're played (Section 4.3.8.3)
- Don't have Strength, Willpower, or Lore values
- Enter play ready (can use abilities immediately)

---

## 6.5. Locations
**Summary:** Cards that represent places, provide abilities, and can be challenged
**Key terms:** location, move cost, willpower, can be challenged, lore value
**Rule refs:** 6.5.1-6.5.8

### Key Rules:
- Locations have "Location" on classification line (6.5.1)
- Have Willpower but no Strength (6.5.2)
- Some locations have Lore Value (◊) - provides lore in Set step (6.5.3)
- Can be challenged by characters (6.5.4)
  - Never ready or exerted, can be challenged anytime
  - Don't deal damage back to challengers
- Have Move Cost - cost to move your character to this location (6.5.5)

### Moving Characters to Locations:
- Pay the move cost to move your character there (Section 4.3.7)
- Can only move your characters to your locations
- Characters "at" a location may gain benefits from location abilities

### Location Parts (6.5.6):
- **Move Cost** - Icon showing cost to move characters there
- **Willpower** - How much damage it can take (like characters)
- **Lore Value** (optional) - Provides lore each turn if present

## Common Questions This Section Answers

**Q: What makes a card a character?**
A: Must have Strength and Willpower, plus a character classification (6.1.2)

**Q: Can I have 4 copies of each version of the same character?**
A: Yes, different versions have different full names (6.2.4, 6.2.5)

**Q: What's the difference between "name" and "full name"?**
A: Name = "Elsa", Full name = "Elsa - Ice Queen" (6.2.4, 6.2.5)

**Q: Can I use an item's ability the turn I play it?**
A: Yes, items can use activated abilities immediately (4.3.8.3)

**Q: Can I challenge a location?**
A: Yes, locations can be challenged and don't deal damage back (6.5.4)

**Q: Do actions go into play?**
A: No, they resolve from hand then go to discard (6.3.1.2)

**Q: How do songs work?**
A: Can pay ink cost normally, or sing by exerting a character (6.3.4)

**Q: What happens if my character is at a location and the location is banished?**
A: Characters at banished locations remain in play, just no longer at a location (6.5.8)
